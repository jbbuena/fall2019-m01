About Los Angeles

A sprawling metropolis, home to a little over 4 million people, and the economic center of Southern California, Los Angeles was originally a Spanish, then Mexican territory until 1847 where it found its way into the U.S. with the conlusion of the Mexican-American war. By the beginning of the 20th Century, L.A. had become home to burgeoning movie industry. In the early 20's, the world's film industry had nearly all been based out os Hollywood. With a booming economy following the end of World War II, Los Angeles' rise has continued well into the present with a vibrant travel economy, flourishing tech industry, and of course, media and entertainment opportunities for every individual looking to chase their dreams in the city of Angels.
